Community-Recommended Food Trips

A Sera Guide to Where to Eat Around the World

If you plan your holidays around what you’re going to eat, you’re in the right place. This guide is built entirely from the Sera community’s own travel recommendations – the places they queue for, dream about on the flight home, and insist their friends book now.

From tiny sandwich counters in Florence to yakitori bars in Tokyo and natural wine spots in Lisbon, this is a living, breathing list of real food lovers’ favourite restaurants, bakeries, bars and markets around the world.

Use it to plan your next food trip, bolt a great meal onto a work trip, or just daydream between bookings.

How to Use This Guide

  • Pick your country or city and scan for the names that keep popping up in our community chats.
  • Expect a mix of high-end tasting menus, local institutions, street food, wine bars, bakeries and coffee spots.
  • Everything here is community-recommended – nothing sponsored, no PR briefs, just places our people genuinely loved.

Latin America: Big Flavours, Big Memories

Argentina – Buenos Aires & Mendoza

Buenos Aires is a dream for anyone who loves beef, wine and late nights.

  • Lo de Jesús – Classic parrilla with perfectly grilled meat and a serious wine list.
  • Anchoita & Anchoita Cava – Frequently called some of the best spots in the city; one for people who love good food and good wine.
  • Gran Dabbang – Fusion plates mixing Indian, Southeast Asian and Argentine flavours.
  • El Preferido de Palermo, Parrilla Peña, Los Galgos and Proper – for old-school steakhouse vibes, tapas, bistro dishes and small plates in a converted garage.
  • In Mendoza and the Maipú wine region, our community rave about Casa Vigil and Azafrán for long lunches, wine and that “we might never go home” feeling.

Chile – Santiago & Atacama

In Santiago, the big-hitters keep coming up:

  • Boragó – A destination tasting menu, rooted in Chilean ingredients.
  • De Raiz and Prima Bar – From the chef behind the now-legendary 99 Restaurante.
  • Bocanariz – A wine bar everyone seems to fall in love with.
  • Emporio Andino – Excellent and must-try empanadas.
  • In San Pedro de Atacama, expectations are lower food-wise (and prices higher) but community mentions include Delicias del Carmen, La Picada del Indio and La Franchuteria – with the top tip being to stay just outside the main town for easier access to activities.

Colombia – Medellín, Bogotá & Cartagena

  • Medellín: Alberto Lechon, Doña Lechona and Sulechona – all about lechona: pork and rice that our members call “incredible” and “unmissable”.
  • Bogotá: Usaquén flea market – Sunday market with excellent artisans, live music and plenty of street food. Also La Rosconería for roscones at breakfast, Libertario and Azahar Café 93 for some of the best coffee in the city, plus Philippe for pastries.
  • Cartagena: Celele (total must-visit), Carmen and Mar y Zielo for modern Caribbean cooking, plus Los Fritos de Dora and La Cevichería for street food and ceviche.

Mexico – CDMX, Oaxaca & Tulum

Mexico City (CDMX) is one of the most-mentioned cities in our entire list.

  • CDMX: Contramar (Tuna tostadas and whole fish are non-negotiable), Campobaja (seafood and oysters with uni). Panadería Rosetta and Rosetta (world-famous bakery and restaurant). Other favorites include Máximo Bistrot, Expendio de Maíz, Hugo el Wine Bar, Taquería Orinoco, Licorería Limantour and Ticuchi.
  • Oaxaca: Expect Alfonsina, Criollo, Casa Oaxaca and El Destilado for memorable meals, plus mezcal bars like In Situ, La Mezcaloteca and Selva.
  • Tulum: Taqueria Honorio (“best tacos in Tulum”), El Rincón Chiapanecos, Taqueria La Chiapaneca, Cervecería Tulum and Chambao also getting regular love.

Europe: Bakeries, Wine Bars & Long Lunches

France – Paris, Nice, Cannes & Toulouse

Paris in our community looks like pastry runs and long lunches:

  • Paris Pastry: Cédric Grolet, The French Bastards, Utopie, Tapisserie and Mamiche.
  • Paris Lunch: Clamato, L’ami Jean, Le Comptoir de la Gastronomie and Grande Brasserie.
  • Paris Evenings: Folderol, Bambino, Clown Bar, Candelaria and Little Red Door.
  • On the Riviera: Nice favourites include Peixes and La Merenda, while in Cannes Astoux et Brun and Hotel Martinez are the classic stops.
  • Toulouse: Victor Hugo Market for oysters, cassoulet at Emile or Le Genty Magre, coffee at Hayuco or Cerise.

Italy – Florence, Rome, Turin, Venice & Tuscany

Pasta, Bistecca, and Roman classics:

  • Florence: I' Girone De' Ghiotti (best sandwich), All’Antico Vinaio, plus Buca Mario, Santo Bevitore, 13 Gobbi and 4 Leoni for bistecca and trattoria classics. La Ménagère is the go-to for breakfast.
  • Rome: Felice a Testaccio, Al Moro, Taverna Trilussa, Roscioli and Pane e Salame all get repeated mentions for pasta, carbonara and long Roman lunches, while Mirabelle is the romantic rooftop choice.
  • Turin & Tuscany: Consorzio in Turin and Trattoria Pane e Vino in Verona are community favourites. Il Lecchio (near Montalcino) and tastings at the Antinori estate in Tuscany.
  • Venice: Venissa appears as the key destination recommendation.

Spain – Barcelona, Madrid, San Sebastián & Málaga

Tapas, pintxos, and modern Spanish plates:

  • Barcelona: Disfrutar/Compartir, Canete, Mano Rota, Martinez, Morro Fi, Bar Brutal, La Plata, El Xampanyet and La Cova Fumada.
  • Madrid: Mercado de San Miguel (grazing), Triciclo, Alabaster and Vinoteca Moratín for modern and classic Spanish menus, and Bodega de los Secretos for the dining experience.
  • San Sebastián: Borda Berri, La Cuchara de San Telmo, Bar Nestor, Casa Urola and La Viña (Basque cheesecake).
  • Málaga & the Balearics: In Málaga, La Cosmopolita, Kaleja, Casa Lola, Uvedoble, La Tranca and El Pimpi headline the tapas crawl. In Palma and Menorca, spots like El Camino, Terrae, Koa, Ses Coves, Sumaq, Sa Llagosta and Trebol Menorca.

Portugal – Lisbon & Porto

Lisbon is a huge community favourite for food trips.

  • Lisbon: Expect O Velho Eurico, Bifanas do Sr. Alonso, Taberna Sal Grosso, Vida de Tasca, Nunes Real Marisqueira, Fogo, SEM and Pigmeu, plus Rio de Mel for serious piri piri chicken.
  • Porto: Favourites include Chama, Cozinha das Flores, Euskalduna, Apego, Almeja, O Afonso, Pedro dos Frangos and seaside stops like Restaurante Veleiros for roasted octopus.

Scandinavia – Copenhagen, Stockholm, Oslo, Helsinki

  • Copenhagen: Bakeries like Juno the Bakery, Meyers Bageri, Hart Bageri and Landbageriet. Big-name restaurants such as Bæst, Kødbyens Fiskebar, Høst, Kadeau, Jordnær, Geranium and Noma.
  • Stockholm: Brasserie Astoria is a big favourite, while Tranan’s latkes, fried herring and off-menu meatballs get rave reviews.
  • Oslo: Kontrast is described as exceptional, with Himkok, Mathallen, Fiskeriet, Tim Wendelboe and Talormade making Oslo a great coffee + seafood + cocktails city.
  • Helsinki: Way Bakery (bakery by day, wine bar with plates by night), Albina, Yes Yes Yes, Seahorse and Harju 8 paint a picture of a small but very considered food scene.

Asia: From Yakitori to Hawker Centres

Japan – Tokyo & Kyoto

Serious sushi and characterful ramen spots:

  • Tokyo: Afuri for yuzu ramen, Ginza Katsukami and Hakkoku for serious tonkatsu and sushi, Ukiyo for refined, spice-led cooking and coffee stops like Torahebi and Mameya Kakeru.
  • Kyoto: Okariba, Wajoryomen Sugari, Hitomi, Hikiniku to Come, Gion Duck Noodles and Vegan Ramen Uzu show a preference for small, characterful spots.

Hong Kong, Singapore, South Korea & Vietnam

  • Hong Kong: Think Yardbird for yakitori and vibes, Mak’s Noodle, Kau Kee and Kam’s Roast Goose for old-school bowls. Big-name dim sum and Cantonese restaurants like The Chairman, Lung King Heen, Tin Lung Heen and Mott 32.
  • Singapore: Our community says to spend as much time as you can at hawker centres, with Odette, Burnt Ends, Candlenut, JAAN, Tippling Club and Imperial Treasure as the higher-end options.
  • South Korea (Seoul): Expect late-night fried chicken at Hanchu, tasting menus at Mingles and Jueun, BBQ at Nari’s House, street food at Gwangjang Market and cocktails at Charles H, OUL and Namsan Sool Club.
  • Vietnam (Saigon & Hoi An): In Ho Chi Minh City, Saigon Street Eats seafood tours, Pho Phuong and Pizza 4P’s all get nods.

Africa & the Middle East: Markets, Tagines & Wine Country

Morocco – Marrakech

Rooftop tagines, hidden courtyards and cocktails:

  • La Famille, +61, Dar Yacout, Sahbi Sahbi, Nomad, Cafe Atay and Chez Lamine all get mentioned, along with Comptoir Darna for a bougie lamb-sharing dinner with belly dancing and El Fenn and Baromètre for drinks.

South Africa – Cape Town & Stellenbosch

From farm markets to fine dining with serious views:

  • Cape Town: Oranjezicht City Farm and Neighbourgoods Market, restaurants like Fyn, La Colombe, Belly of the Beast, Ouzeri, Emazulwini and The Pot Luck Club.
  • Stellenbosch: Delaire Graff, Tokara, Rust en Vrede, Dusk, Overture and Eike all appear as memorable meals with serious views and wine.

North America: From NY Slices to LA Tacos

United States Highlights

  • New York City & Brooklyn: The list runs from Katz’s Deli, Russ & Daughters, Joe’s Shanghai and Prince Street Pizza to Lucali, Oxomoco, Peter Luger and Maison Premiere.
  • Los Angeles: Community favourites include Courage Bagels, Teddy’s Red Tacos, Bestia, Bavel, République, Sqirl, Gjelina, Jitlada, Soowon Galbi, Parks BBQ, Guelaguetza, Grand Central Market and Smorgasburg LA, plus an extensive roster of sushi and kaiseki spots such as Sushi Zo and n/naka.
  • San Francisco: Recommendations include Zuni Café (always order the roast chicken and bread salad), House of Prime Rib, Harris’ Steakhouse and Mister Jiu’s.
  • Miami: Mixes Cuban institutions like Versailles, El Sanguich, Cafe La Trova and Tinta y Cafe with sushi at Uchi and Hiyakawa.

The UK & Ireland: Weekenders Worth Booking

UK & Ireland Highlights

  • UK Favourites: Range from Wheelers and Whitstable Oyster Company in Whitstable to Roots and The Star Inn in Yorkshire. Country pubs like The Bull, The Swan, The Lamb Inn and The Bell in the Cotswolds. Manchester spots such as Psycho Sandbar, El Gato Negro, Kala, Mackie Mayor and deep dish at Ramonas.
  • Ireland: Paradiso in Cork (seasonal vegetarian food), Elbow Lane and Market Lane for smokehouse and modern Irish plates, and West-coast stops like The Misunderstood Heron hut and An Port Mór for views with your seafood.
