    <!-- LATIN AMERICA -->

    <section class="mb-16">

      <h2 class="section-header text-sera-gold">Latin America: Big Flavours, Big Memories</h2>

      <h3 class="city-name">Argentina – Buenos Aires & Mendoza</h3>

      <p class="text-gray-400 mb-4">Buenos Aires is a dream for anyone who loves beef, wine and late nights.</p>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li><b>Lo de Jesús</b> – Classic parrilla with perfectly grilled meat and a serious wine list.</li>

        <li><b>Anchoita & Anchoita Cava</b> – Frequently called some of the best spots in the city; one for people who love good food and good wine.</li>

        <li><b>Gran Dabbang</b> – Fusion plates mixing Indian, Southeast Asian and Argentine flavours.</li>

        <li><b>El Preferido de Palermo, Parrilla Peña, Los Galgos and Proper</b> – for old-school steakhouse vibes, tapas, bistro dishes and small plates in a converted garage.</li>

        <li>In Mendoza and the Maipú wine region, our community rave about <b>Casa Vigil</b> and <b>Azafrán</b> for long lunches, wine and that “we might never go home” feeling.</li>

      </ul>

      <h3 class="city-name">Chile – Santiago & Atacama</h3>

      <p class="text-gray-400 mb-4">In Santiago, the big-hitters keep coming up:</p>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li><b>Boragó</b> – A destination tasting menu, rooted in Chilean ingredients.</li>

        <li><b>De Raiz and Prima Bar</b> – From the chef behind the now-legendary 99 Restaurante.</li>

        <li><b>Bocanariz</b> – A wine bar everyone seems to fall in love with.</li>

        <li><b>Emporio Andino</b> – Excellent and must-try empanadas.</li>

        <li>In San Pedro de Atacama, expectations are lower food-wise (and prices higher) but community mentions include <b>Delicias del Carmen, La Picada del Indio</b> and <b>La Franchuteria</b> – with the top tip being to stay just outside the main town for easier access to activities.</li>

      </ul>

      <h3 class="city-name">Colombia – Medellín, Bogotá & Cartagena</h3>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>Medellín: <b>Alberto Lechon, Doña Lechona</b> and <b>Sulechona</b> – all about lechona: pork and rice that our members call “incredible” and “unmissable”.</li>

        <li>Bogotá: <b>Usaquén flea market</b> – Sunday market with excellent artisans, live music and plenty of street food. Also <b>La Rosconería</b> for roscones at breakfast, <b>Libertario</b> and <b>Azahar Café 93</b> for some of the best coffee in the city, plus <b>Philippe</b> for pastries.</li>

        <li>Cartagena: <b>Celele</b> (total must-visit), <b>Carmen</b> and <b>Mar y Zielo</b> for modern Caribbean cooking, plus <b>Los Fritos de Dora</b> and <b>La Cevichería</b> for street food and ceviche.</li>

      </ul>

      <h3 class="city-name">Mexico – CDMX, Oaxaca & Tulum</h3>

      <p class="text-gray-400 mb-4">Mexico City (CDMX) is one of the most-mentioned cities in our entire list.</p>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>CDMX: <b>Contramar</b> (Tuna tostadas and whole fish are non-negotiable), <b>Campobaja</b> (seafood and oysters with uni). <b>Panadería Rosetta and Rosetta</b> (world-famous bakery and restaurant). Other favorites include <b>Máximo Bistrot, Expendio de Maíz, Hugo el Wine Bar, Taquería Orinoco, Licorería Limantour</b> and <b>Ticuchi</b>.</li>

        <li>Oaxaca: Expect <b>Alfonsina, Criollo, Casa Oaxaca</b> and <b>El Destilado</b> for memorable meals, plus mezcal bars like <b>In Situ, La Mezcaloteca</b> and <b>Selva</b>.</li>

        <li>Tulum: <b>Taqueria Honorio</b> (“best tacos in Tulum”), <b>El Rincón Chiapanecos, Taqueria La Chiapaneca, Cervecería Tulum</b> and <b>Chambao</b> also getting regular love.</li>

      </ul>

    </section>


    <!-- EUROPE -->

    <section class="mb-16">

      <h2 class="section-header text-sera-gold">Europe: Bakeries, Wine Bars & Long Lunches</h2>

      <h3 class="city-name">France – Paris, Nice, Cannes & Toulouse</h3>

      <p class="text-gray-400 mb-4">Paris in our community looks like pastry runs and long lunches:</p>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>Paris Pastry: <b>Cédric Grolet, The French Bastards, Utopie, Tapisserie</b> and <b>Mamiche</b>.</li>

        <li>Paris Lunch: <b>Clamato, L’ami Jean, Le Comptoir de la Gastronomie</b> and <b>Grande Brasserie</b>.</li>

        <li>Paris Evenings: <b>Folderol, Bambino, Clown Bar, Candelaria</b> and <b>Little Red Door</b>.</li>

        <li>On the Riviera: Nice favourites include <b>Peixes</b> and <b>La Merenda</b>, while in Cannes <b>Astoux et Brun</b> and <b>Hotel Martinez</b> are the classic stops.</li>

        <li>Toulouse: <b>Victor Hugo Market</b> for oysters, cassoulet at <b>Emile</b> or <b>Le Genty Magre</b>, coffee at <b>Hayuco</b> or <b>Cerise</b>.</li>

      </ul>

      <h3 class="city-name">Italy – Florence, Rome, Turin, Venice & Tuscany</h3>

      <p class="text-gray-400 mb-4">Pasta, Bistecca, and Roman classics:</p>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>Florence: <b>I' Girone De' Ghiotti</b> (best sandwich), <b>All’Antico Vinaio</b>, plus <b>Buca Mario, Santo Bevitore, 13 Gobbi</b> and <b>4 Leoni</b> for bistecca and trattoria classics. <b>La Ménagère</b> is the go-to for breakfast.</li>

        <li>Rome: <b>Felice a Testaccio, Al Moro, Taverna Trilussa, Roscioli</b> and <b>Pane e Salame</b> all get repeated mentions for pasta, carbonara and long Roman lunches, while <b>Mirabelle</b> is the romantic rooftop choice.</li>

        <li>Turin & Tuscany: <b>Consorzio</b> in Turin and <b>Trattoria Pane e Vino</b> in Verona are community favourites. <b>Il Lecchio</b> (near Montalcino) and tastings at the <b>Antinori estate</b> in Tuscany.</li>

        <li>Venice: <b>Venissa</b> appears as the key destination recommendation.</li>

      </ul>

      <h3 class="city-name">Spain – Barcelona, Madrid, San Sebastián & Málaga</h3>

      <p class="text-gray-400 mb-4">Tapas, pintxos, and modern Spanish plates:</p>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>Barcelona: <b>Disfrutar/Compartir, Canete, Mano Rota, Martinez, Morro Fi, Bar Brutal, La Plata, El Xampanyet</b> and <b>La Cova Fumada</b>.</li>

        <li>Madrid: <b>Mercado de San Miguel</b> (grazing), <b>Triciclo, Alabaster</b> and <b>Vinoteca Moratín</b> for modern and classic Spanish menus, and <b>Bodega de los Secretos</b> for the dining experience.</li>

        <li>San Sebastián: <b>Borda Berri, La Cuchara de San Telmo, Bar Nestor, Casa Urola</b> and <b>La Viña</b> (Basque cheesecake).</li>

        <li>Málaga & the Balearics: In Málaga, <b>La Cosmopolita, Kaleja, Casa Lola, Uvedoble, La Tranca</b> and <b>El Pimpi</b> headline the tapas crawl. In Palma and Menorca, spots like <b>El Camino, Terrae, Koa, Ses Coves, Sumaq, Sa Llagosta</b> and <b>Trebol Menorca</b>.</li>

      </ul>

      <h3 class="city-name">Portugal – Lisbon & Porto</h3>

      <p class="text-gray-400 mb-4">Lisbon is a huge community favourite for food trips.</p>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>Lisbon: Expect <b>O Velho Eurico, Bifanas do Sr. Alonso, Taberna Sal Grosso, Vida de Tasca, Nunes Real Marisqueira, Fogo, SEM</b> and <b>Pigmeu</b>, plus <b>Rio de Mel</b> for serious piri piri chicken.</li>

        <li>Porto: Favourites include <b>Chama, Cozinha das Flores, Euskalduna, Apego, Almeja, O Afonso, Pedro dos Frangos</b> and seaside stops like <b>Restaurante Veleiros</b> for roasted octopus.</li>

      </ul>

      <h3 class="city-name">Scandinavia – Copenhagen, Stockholm, Oslo, Helsinki</h3>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>Copenhagen: Bakeries like <b>Juno the Bakery, Meyers Bageri, Hart Bageri</b> and <b>Landbageriet</b>. Big-name restaurants such as <b>Bæst, Kødbyens Fiskebar, Høst, Kadeau, Jordnær, Geranium</b> and <b>Noma</b>.</li>

        <li>Stockholm: <b>Brasserie Astoria</b> is a big favourite, while <b>Tranan’s</b> latkes, fried herring and off-menu meatballs get rave reviews.</li>

        <li>Oslo: <b>Kontrast</b> is described as exceptional, with <b>Himkok, Mathallen, Fiskeriet, Tim Wendelboe</b> and <b>Talormade</b> making Oslo a great coffee + seafood + cocktails city.</li>

        <li>Helsinki: <b>Way Bakery</b> (bakery by day, wine bar with plates by night), <b>Albina, Yes Yes Yes, Seahorse</b> and <b>Harju 8</b> paint a picture of a small but very considered food scene.</li>

      </ul>

    </section>

    

    <!-- ASIA -->

    <section class="mb-16">

      <h2 class="section-header text-sera-gold">Asia: From Yakitori to Hawker Centres</h2>

      <h3 class="city-name">Japan – Tokyo & Kyoto</h3>

      <p class="text-gray-400 mb-4">Serious sushi and characterful ramen spots:</p>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>Tokyo: <b>Afuri</b> for yuzu ramen, <b>Ginza Katsukami</b> and <b>Hakkoku</b> for serious tonkatsu and sushi, <b>Ukiyo</b> for refined, spice-led cooking and coffee stops like <b>Torahebi</b> and <b>Mameya Kakeru</b>.</li>

        <li>Kyoto: <b>Okariba, Wajoryomen Sugari, Hitomi, Hikiniku to Come, Gion Duck Noodles</b> and <b>Vegan Ramen Uzu</b> show a preference for small, characterful spots.</li>

      </ul>

      <h3 class="city-name">Hong Kong, Singapore, South Korea & Vietnam</h3>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>Hong Kong: Think <b>Yardbird</b> for yakitori and vibes, <b>Mak’s Noodle, Kau Kee</b> and <b>Kam’s Roast Goose</b> for old-school bowls. Big-name dim sum and Cantonese restaurants like <b>The Chairman, Lung King Heen, Tin Lung Heen</b> and <b>Mott 32</b>.</li>

        <li>Singapore: Our community says to spend as much time as you can at <b>hawker centres</b>, with <b>Odette, Burnt Ends, Candlenut, JAAN, Tippling Club</b> and <b>Imperial Treasure</b> as the higher-end options.</li>

        <li>South Korea (Seoul): Expect late-night fried chicken at <b>Hanchu</b>, tasting menus at <b>Mingles</b> and <b>Jueun</b>, BBQ at <b>Nari’s House</b>, street food at <b>Gwangjang Market</b> and cocktails at <b>Charles H, OUL</b> and <b>Namsan Sool Club</b>.</li>

        <li>Vietnam (Saigon & Hoi An): In Ho Chi Minh City, <b>Saigon Street Eats</b> seafood tours, <b>Pho Phuong</b> and <b>Pizza 4P’s</b> all get nods.</li>

      </ul>

    </section>


    <!-- AFRICA & MIDDLE EAST -->

    <section class="mb-16">

      <h2 class="section-header text-sera-gold">Africa & the Middle East: Markets, Tagines & Wine Country</h2>

      <h3 class="city-name">Morocco – Marrakech</h3>

      <p class="text-gray-400 mb-4">Rooftop tagines, hidden courtyards and cocktails:</p>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li><b>La Famille, +61, Dar Yacout, Sahbi Sahbi, Nomad, Cafe Atay</b> and <b>Chez Lamine</b> all get mentioned, along with <b>Comptoir Darna</b> for a bougie lamb-sharing dinner with belly dancing and <b>El Fenn</b> and <b>Baromètre</b> for drinks.</li>

      </ul>

      <h3 class="city-name">South Africa – Cape Town & Stellenbosch</h3>

      <p class="text-gray-400 mb-4">From farm markets to fine dining with serious views:</p>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>Cape Town: <b>Oranjezicht City Farm</b> and <b>Neighbourgoods Market</b>, restaurants like <b>Fyn, La Colombe, Belly of the Beast, Ouzeri, Emazulwini</b> and <b>The Pot Luck Club</b>.</li>

        <li>Stellenbosch: <b>Delaire Graff, Tokara, Rust en Vrede, Dusk, Overture</b> and <b>Eike</b> all appear as memorable meals with serious views and wine.</li>

      </ul>

    </section>


    <!-- NORTH AMERICA -->

    <section class="mb-16">

      <h2 class="section-header text-sera-gold">North America: From NY Slices to LA Tacos</h2>

      <h3 class="city-name">United States Highlights</h3>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>New York City & Brooklyn: The list runs from <b>Katz’s Deli, Russ & Daughters, Joe’s Shanghai</b> and <b>Prince Street Pizza</b> to <b>Lucali, Oxomoco, Peter Luger</b> and <b>Maison Premiere</b>.</li>

        <li>Los Angeles: Community favourites include <b>Courage Bagels, Teddy’s Red Tacos, Bestia, Bavel, République, Sqirl, Gjelina, Jitlada, Soowon Galbi, Parks BBQ, Guelaguetza, Grand Central Market</b> and <b>Smorgasburg LA</b>, plus an extensive roster of sushi and kaiseki spots such as <b>Sushi Zo</b> and <b>n/naka</b>.</li>

        <li>San Francisco: Recommendations include <b>Zuni Café</b> (always order the roast chicken and bread salad), <b>House of Prime Rib, Harris’ Steakhouse</b> and <b>Mister Jiu’s</b>.</li>

        <li>Miami: Mixes Cuban institutions like <b>Versailles, El Sanguich, Cafe La Trova</b> and <b>Tinta y Cafe</b> with sushi at <b>Uchi</b> and <b>Hiyakawa</b>.</li>

      </ul>

    </section>


    <!-- UK & IRELAND -->

    <section class="mb-16">

      <h2 class="section-header text-sera-gold">The UK & Ireland: Weekenders Worth Booking</h2>

      <h3 class="city-name">UK & Ireland Highlights</h3>

      <ul class="list-disc ml-6 space-y-2 text-gray-300 place-list">

        <li>UK Favourites: Range from <b>Wheelers</b> and <b>Whitstable Oyster Company</b> in Whitstable to <b>Roots</b> and <b>The Star Inn</b> in Yorkshire. Country pubs like <b>The Bull, The Swan, The Lamb Inn</b> and <b>The Bell</b> in the Cotswolds. Manchester spots such as <b>Psycho Sandbar, El Gato Negro, Kala, Mackie Mayor</b> and deep dish at <b>Ramonas</b>.</li>

        <li>Ireland: <b>Paradiso</b> in Cork (seasonal vegetarian food), <b>Elbow Lane</b> and <b>Market Lane</b> for smokehouse and modern Irish plates, and West-coast stops like <b>The Misunderstood Heron hut</b> and <b>An Port Mór</b> for views with your seafood.</li>

      </ul>
